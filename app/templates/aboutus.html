Biases and Limitations

In analyzing bear attack incidents, several biases may affect the dataset. Severe or fatal attacks are more likely to be reported, while minor incidents often go unrecorded. This reporting bias skews the data toward more extreme cases, with survivor bias further contributing by underrepresenting non-fatal encounters. Additionally, the data is geographically concentrated, limiting broader insights across regions. There is also the possibility of human error due to manual data entry, which could introduce inaccuracies.

The analysis is further constrained by certain limitations. Some incidents have incomplete data, missing key details such as dates, locations, or the circumstances surrounding the attacks. The dataset also includes older records that may lack consistency or detail compared to recent entries. Variability in data formats across sources necessitates standardization efforts, adding complexity to the analysis. Additionally, the dataset does not provide enough information to establish direct causes of bear attacks. Finally, the focus on polar bear attacks limits the ability to generalize findings to other species.
