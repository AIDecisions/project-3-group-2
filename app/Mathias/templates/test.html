Jaws vs. Claws: A Data-Driven Exploration

Bridging the gap between fiction and reality with real-world data.

Project Motivation

Our team has a strong interest in nature and wildlife, and this fascination has been further fueled by iconic films like "Jaws" and "The Revenant." In "Jaws," we see a dramatic portrayal of a shark terrorizing a small town, which captures the intense and often terrifying nature of human-shark encounters. On the other hand, "The Revenant" offers a raw and gripping depiction of bear attacks in the wilderness, showcasing the power and unpredictability of these animals.

These cinematic portrayals, while compelling, are dramatized for entertainment. To get a clearer, more factual picture of how these interactions play out in reality, we’re turning to real-world data. By analyzing datasets on bear and shark attacks, we aim to bridge the gap between fiction and fact. We’re interested in understanding how often these encounters occur, the conditions under which they happen, and what commonalities or differences exist between them.

Our goal is to move beyond the sensationalized stories and get a data-driven perspective on these wildlife interactions. This will not only satisfy our curiosity but also provide valuable insights into animal behavior and human safety.

Research Guiding Questions

Guiding Questions
  • How have bear and shark attacks changed over the years?
  • Where are the main spots for these attacks?
  • Who are the typical victims in these attacks?

These questions will help guide our project, and we’ll use our dashboard and maps to answer them.
